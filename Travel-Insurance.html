When taking a vacation, there are two kinds of insurance options to consider. Travel medical insurance is best for those traveling outside the country who want medical coverage. Many travelers assume their existing medical insurance will cover them for injuries and illnesses abroad, but usually those traditional plans are designed only to offer domestic coverage.

You can also consider travel insurance, also known as a travel protection plan, which is meant to protect your financial investment. These plans can provide coverage for prepaid, non-refundable trip costs, and some also offer limited medical benefits.

With travel insurance, you can embrace your wanderlust without worrying about the "what ifs." Whether it’s a last-minute cancellation, a medical emergency, or a lost suitcase, we’ve got you covered. So pack your bags, set your out-of-office message, and get ready to explore the world with confidence. Your journey deserves to be as smooth and stress-free as possible—let us handle the rest.

Do I Need Insurance for My Vacation?

Even the most careful travelers aren't immune to car accidents, natural disasters, political unrest, and other unforeseen events.

What Factors Should I Consider?

Travel Expenses

Could you afford to lose your non-refundable, prepaid travel expenses in the case of your trip being cancelled due to an unforeseen event (e.g., death of a family member)?

Severe Weather

Are you traveling to a destination that may be affected by severe weather?

Out-of-Pocket Costs

If you were to get sick or injured on the trip, would you be unable to pay out of pocket for the necessary health care?

Flight Delays

If your flight is delayed, would you seek reimbursement for the additional charges such as change fees?

Lost or Stolen Items

If your luggage is lost or stolen, could you afford to replace your belongings?
